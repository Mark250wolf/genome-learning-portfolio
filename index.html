ðŸ”¬ CRISPR-Cas9 Overview

CRISPR-Cas9 is a genome editing system that allows scientists to precisely modify DNA sequences. The Cas9 enzyme, guided by RNA, identifies a specific DNA target sequence and introduces a double-strand break. The cell then repairs the break through either Non-Homologous End Joining (NHEJ) or Homology-Directed Repair (HDR).

Understanding CRISPR requires knowledge of:

  • DNA structure and base pairing
  • PAM sequences
  • Guide RNA design
  • DNA repair mechanisms
  • Chemical bond interactions within biomolecules
